What is...

XBRL
eXtensible Business Reporting Language. Example (Google's 2012 Annual Report). Not very reader-friendly. But provides rich business data: accounts (i.e. Cash), dates (i.e. 2013), and units (i.e. USD).
inline
Describes anything written inside HTML. Like an inline style (styles control colors, sizes, and positions) or an inline script (scripts control interactions).
HTML
HyperText Markup Language. What you see in your internet browser. Examples include almost everything you see and use on the web.
iXBRL
inline XBRL. XBRL written inside HTML, so you can see it in your browser. Very reader-friendly. But no different than HTML. Hard to use the rich business data.
iQ
inline XBRL Query (Language). A way to use the rich business data. Examples include: finding, creating, comparing, and validating.

Why should I use...

Browsers
They are free. There are many choices. They connect you to the web. They are powerful, fast, beautiful and always-improving.
HTML
It is standard. It is international. Browsers recognize it. Schools teach it. Businesses use it. HTML can express most kinds of human documents/artifacts.
XBRL
Like HTML, XBRL is standard. Like HTML, XBRL is international. But browsers don't recognize XBRL in meaningful ways.
iXBRL
Create documents for browser-viewing with HTML, but add meaningful business information with XBRL. Send it to anyone with a Browser, and it's at least as good as a PDF, a Word Document, and a plain old website.
iQ
Makes iXBRL far better than a PDF, a Word Document, a website, an Excel Spreadsheet, and a Powerpoint document. Combined. Free to use, free to consume, easy to learn.

How does it work...

iXBRL

Business Info

Business data can be diverse. But it must express basic information. iXBRL can express these things:

Concept

What is it?

Examples: Cash. Number of Subscribers. Taxes Due. Rental Rate.

AKA: Element. Tag. Account. Line Item.

Date

When is it?

Examples: The year ending 2013. The first three months of 2014. On April 11th, 2013.

AKA: Instant. Duration. Time span. Point in time.

Unit

How is it measured?

Examples: US Dollars ($). Euro (â‚¬). Square Feet. Barrels of Oil. Customers.

AKA: Denomination.

iXBRL can express other business information. Like Accounting Debits and Credits. Or Business Segments (i.e. European sales vs American sales). But let's start with the basics.

iQ

Finding

The core of iQ is finding.

The "Q" in "iQ" stands for "Query". And that's how you find business value(s): writing simple Queries.

Queries suggest SQL which work with databases and need special computers and super-smart people.

But iQ is more like jQuery and works with documents and need only your browser and a willingness to learn.
