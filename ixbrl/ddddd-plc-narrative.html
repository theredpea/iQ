At the AGM in January 2005, Shareholders approved the Encouraging Executive Share Ownership Scheme (EESOS). The EESOS was introduced to encourage executive directors and senior executives within the DDDDD PLC group of companies (the Group) to hold shares in the Company.


No invitations to acquire Bonus Shares under the EESOS may be granted after 16 January 2015


The key aspects of the EESOS are given below.


Annual bonus in shares


Under the EESOS participants may if the Committee so determines, be invited to invest all or part of their net (after tax) annual bonus in any year in shares in the Company (Bonus Shares), subject to a minimum investment of 1,000 shares. The participant will choose how much of the net bonus to invest and that amount is then used to purchase shares in the Company. Invitations to acquire Bonus Shares may be made at any time except when a director would be prohibited from dealing in shares under the Model Code.


Matching share awards


If Bonus Shares are retained by the participant, and the participant remains employed within the Group for three years from the date of acquisition of the Bonus Shares, the Committee may provide additional shares in the Company (Matching Shares). The number of Matching Shares will be on a basis no greater than one for one to the number of Bonus Shares held by the participant at the end of that three-year period. Matching share awards are not transferable or pensionable.


To date the Committee has operated a policy of limiting a matching share award under the Scheme to no more than 20,000 shares per individual each year (which would match the individual's own investment of his annual cash bonus in Bonus Shares).


Certain executives were invited to invest all or part of their net (after tax) bonus in Company shares.  If the executive then remains employed within the group for three years from the date of acquisition of these shares the Company will transfer additional shares to the executive on a one-for-one basis


During the year to 30 September 2009 111,800 new shares were purchased by executives under the terms of the above scheme and 4,500 shares were transferred to executives who qualified for the additional shares based on previous purchases during the period ended 1 October 2005. The cumulative total of shares purchased under the scheme and still held by executives as at 30 September 2009 was 137,163.

No Award may be granted under the LTSP 2008 after 24 February 2018.

Individual Limits


The maximum number of shares that may be awarded to a participant in any 12 month period will be limited so that the market value of such shares on the date of grant will not normally exceed 100 per cent of basic salary. Performance conditions


Payment of Awards is subject to attainment of targets set by the Committee at the time of grant which require demanding and stretching financial achievement for a fixed three-year period beginning with that financial year (the Performance Period). Once set, performance targets may not be varied by the Committee, unless the Committee reasonably considers it to be necessary to ensure that the effectiveness of the Award as an incentive is not undermined. For the Awards made in 2008, the Committee set a target for real growth in the EPS. There is no provision to retest performance.

Chairman's Statement


DDDDD now comprises three cash generative and profitable businesses, Division A, Division B and Division C, of which Division A and Division B are particularly well placed in their markets; the group has been refinanced, well ahead of any deadlines and is facing the business challenges which are normal in tough times with gusto. As the economy moves out of recession, we believe we are well-positioned to grow again by organic investment and selective acquisitions should circumstances allow. In the meantime the businesses are being managed tightly and effectively within the constraints imposed by the recession and DDDDD's recent circumstances. The Chief Executive's review has more detail on our plans for the future of the businesses and why we believe they are well placed in their respective markets.


A very significant amount of shareholder value was unfortunately destroyed in the demise of Division D and, although our ability to resist was exceedingly limited despite extremely competitive tendering and our very best efforts, we sincerely regret the value destruction.

Chief Executive's Review of the Business



The demise of the gadget business


2009 was clearly an horrendous year for the DDDDD group. It is generally accepted that the extraordinary accomplishment of rationalising a sizeable organisation within so short a timescale is very largely due to the professionalism of DDDDD's staff. Although there are too many people to name individually, it would be remiss of me not to acknowledge and thank them for their steadfast dedication and commitment in the face of considerable personal uncertainty.


I can only echo the Chairman's description of the regret and sadness at these events despite our best endeavours, the powers at work were too great to be resisted and to save the gadget business. However we did preserve value through rescuing the other three businesses and it is upon the surviving group which we now concentrate our efforts. The remainder of this summary addresses those businesses which are DDDDD's future.


Overview


The results of the continuing businesses each reflect the unprecedented trading environment - and the effects of DDDDD Subsidiary 5 Limited's administration. While the rapid and effective legal and commercial actions taken to protect and reorganise the continuing businesses countered most of the substantive risk by 28 May, customers and suppliers inevitably remained nervous about DDDDD's survival well beyond that point. As we continue to trade profitably, that period of uncertainty seems now to be receding and further certainty has been provided by the completion of the refinancing.


Division C, where clients small widget spend is the source of much of its business, is the division most immediately exposed to the economic cycle and indeed it is there that we experienced the worst of the recession's impact.  It is testament to the responsiveness of the Division C business that it remained profitable in the face of a sudden and dramatic rise in dollar-denominated widget pricing. Equally, Division A was able to adjust, particularly in the UK, to mitigate the damaging effects of the volume decline in small widgets.


Financial highlights


Accounting for the events of the past year is complex and, in a number of instances hugely counter-intuitive. The group accounting for the solvency of DDDDD Subsidiary 5 could not be further from the commercial reality - an item we are required to describe as a profit on de-recognition represents the removal of the net liabilities of the News business for no cash value and for the benefit of no-one, but more pertinently also the destruction of a previously healthy enterprise, employing some 2,500 people. The Financial Review deals with the particular technicalities of the financial statements. The uncomplicated comparison of trading results for each of the three businesses shows the effects of the economic climate with the impact on Division A and Division C being particularly difficult in the second half of the year.


By the end of the reporting period, the group had moved to the situation where it has positive net assets. The smaller group has a much simpler balance sheet with commensurately lower levels of indebtedness, providing a solid base upon which to move forward.

Corporate Social Responsibility Report


This Corporate Social Responsibility Report considers issues related to employees, the environment and the community.


The Board is committed to upholding good practice as regards social, environmental and ethical matters in the conduct of the Company's business.


The Board has assigned overall Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) to Jim Ash, the Chief Executive and, to give this effect has set up a CSR Committee comprising the Chief Executive, the Company Secretary and those senior executives with responsibility for aspects of CSR. The Committee reports to the Board on a monthly basis for Health & Safety and a quarterly basis for all other matters.


Employee considerations

The three key considerations are Health and Safety, Equal Opportunities and Employee Communication and Consultation.


Health and Safety


The Board of Directors fully accepts that it is the Board's responsibility to seek to ensure the health, safety and welfare of all employees and others that may be affected by the group's work activities. The Chief Executive has overall responsibility to ensure all policies relating to health and safety at work are Implemented and maintained.


The Health and Safety policy statement is reviewed annually and updated as necessary. The key principles of the policy are that:


all practical steps are taken to safeguard the health safety and welfare of all employees, visitors and contractors to all premises or operations within our control;


employees are provided with such information, instruction, training and supervision as are reasonably necessary to secure health and safety at work;


employees are made aware that they should take reasonable steps for the health and safety of themselves and any other person who may be affected by their acts or omissions; and


the group remains committed to consultation with employees over health, safety and welfare matters and, by way of promoting this employee involvement the group has established safety committees and actively encourages employees from all areas of the group to assist managers during regular local safety inspections


The group has comprehensive procedures and documentation which, together with the policy statement, are regularly reviewed and updated. This framework has been endorsed and commended by the statutory authorities.


The policy and procedures include processes to reduce risk in five critical areas: unloading and loading, forklift truck operations, slips and trips, manual handling and delivery.


Risk assessments are produced for each of the five areas and, from those assessments, action points are identified and communicated to employees.


Ensuring that the equipment used in the business is suitable for the task is key to reducing risks in the work place. Forklift truck and vehicle maintenance are monitored and the weight of loads is checked to ensure that delivery vehicles are within legal limits.


Health and safety audits, inspections and reviews have taken place at all locations and extensive, detailed monitoring and reviewing of records against measurable targets are carried out.


Equal opportunities


The Board is committed to a policy of treating employees and job applicants fairly and to promoting equality of opportunity within the group. In order to achieve this objective we aim to:


make judgements and decisions concerning such areas as recruitment, payment, training, promotion, work allocation, treatment and selection for redundancy, discipline, capability and dismissal based solely on suitability for the job;


ensure that all applicants and employees receive fair and equal treatment regarding the above irrespective of colour, race nationality, ethnic or national origin, sex, marital status, disability, trade union membership, part-time working, sexual orientation, age, social status, family responsibilities and religious belief;


maintain a working environment free from harassment and intimidation; and


ensure that all employment conditions and job requirements reflect our commitment to equal opportunities.


In addition, employees are actively encouraged to develop their skills and qualifications and to participate in continual personal development through a range of training and education opportunities.


Employee communication and consultation


The Company values the benefits of employee communication and consultation and has established across the group a structure of employee forums. The framework is based on a series of Local Employee Forums comprising elected representatives and these feed into a National Forum. The purpose is to focus on the employee aspects of business strategy, to encourage employee participation and improve communication both to and from our employees.


Environment


The Board is committed to environmental protection and the development of processes that will make a continuing contribution to that goal. This is achieved by:


encouraging customers and suppliers to support our objectives in their business relationships with us;


aiming continuously to minimise the volume of paper utilised in the group and wherever possible to recycle paper internally;


reviewing our energy usage by conducting energy surveys at all sites. It is our aim to ensure water and electricity are used efficiently in both operational and non operational areas;


managing our environmental impacts particularly regarding the prevention of pollution by operating, as a minimum, within current environmental legislation and continually developing our environmental management processes;


wherever possible using environmentally safe and sustainable resources;


educating, training and motivating employees so they are aware of the potential impact of their actions on the environment and to ensure they are fully aware of the Environmental policy; and


ensuring those who act on our behalf for waste disposal are competent to do so, discharge their duties correctly and are able to prove this by means of the appropriate documentation and checks.

Remuneration Policy


The remuneration policy is designed to attract, retain and motivate executive directors of the highest quality, and reward them in a way that encourages the creation of shareholder value. The Committee intends remuneration to be strongly linked to performance, with variable pay making up a significant proportion of the total package.


The five main elements of executive directors' remuneration are:


Basic salary

Performance-related bonus

Encouraging Executive Share Ownership Scheme

Long term incentive

Pension


Each of these elements is described below.


Basic Salary


Each executive director's salary is reviewed annually by the Committee effective from 1 July to ensure it is appropriate and competitive for the responsibilities involved having regard to the individual's performance and market levels. Market levels are determined by reference to the practice of companies of comparable size, sector and complexity to DDDDD PLC.


Performance-Related Bonus


The level of annual performance bonus is mainly based upon the achievement of Earnings Per Share (EPS) with the remainder based on the achievement of personal goals. The bonus for Jim Ash is subject to a cap of 50% and that for Steve Oak to a cap of 40% of their respective average basic salary in the reporting period. The bonus cap for Tom White was 65%. The Committee reviews this scheme annually.


Encouraging Executive Share Ownership Scheme


At the AGM in January 2005, Shareholders approved the Encouraging Executive Share Ownership Scheme (EESOS). The EESOS was introduced to encourage executive directors and senior executives within the DDDDD PLC group of companies (the Group) to hold shares in the Company.


No invitations to acquire Bonus Shares under the EESOS may be granted after 16 January 2015


The key aspects of the EESOS are given below.


Annual bonus in shares


Under the EESOS participants may if the Committee so determines, be invited to invest all or part of their net (after tax) annual bonus in any year in shares in the Company (Bonus Shares), subject to a minimum investment of 1,000 shares. The participant will choose how much of the net bonus to invest and that amount is then used to purchase shares in the Company. Invitations to acquire Bonus Shares may be made at any time except when a director would be prohibited from dealing in shares under the Model Code.


Matching share awards


If Bonus Shares are retained by the participant, and the participant remains employed within the Group for three years from the date of acquisition of the Bonus Shares, the Committee may provide additional shares in the Company (Matching Shares). The number of Matching Shares will be on a basis no greater than one for one to the number of Bonus Shares held by the participant at the end of that three-year period. Matching share awards are not transferable or pensionable.


To date the Committee has operated a policy of limiting a matching share award under the Scheme to no more than 20,000 shares per individual each year (which would match the individual's own investment of his annual cash bonus in Bonus Shares).


The Long Term Share Plan 2008


The Long Term Share Plan 2008 (LTSP 2008) was approved by the shareholders at the AGM in February 2008. The LTSP 2008 was introduced to ensure that long-term incentives have clear and stretching targets, provide strong alignment with shareholders, are tailored to the Company's circumstances and are cost effective.


No Award may be granted under the LTSP 2008 after 24 February 2018.


The key aspects of the LTSP 2008 are given below.


Individual Limits


The maximum number of shares that may be awarded to a participant in any 12 month period will be limited so that the market value of such shares on the date of grant will not normally exceed 100 per cent of basic salary. However, Awards over shares with a market value of up to 200 per cent of basic salary may be made if the Committee considers that exceptional circumstances exist that justify a higher award.


Performance conditions


Payment of Awards is subject to attainment of targets set by the Committee at the time of grant which require demanding and stretching financial achievement for a fixed three-year period beginning with that financial year (the Performance Period). Once set, performance targets may not be varied by the Committee, unless the Committee reasonably considers it to be necessary to ensure that the effectiveness of the Award as an incentive is not undermined. For the Awards made in 2008, the Committee set a target for real growth in the EPS. There is no provision to retest performance.


Long Term Incentive Plan 2006


At the AGM in January 2006 Shareholders approved the Long Term Incentive Plan 2006 (LTIP 2006). The LTIP 2006 was introduced to incentivise executive directors and senior executives to contribute to the long-term growth of the Company and to encourage share ownership. In any year in which an executive receives an Award under the LTSP 2008 no additional Awards will be made to him or her under the LTIP 2006. No Award may be granted under the LTlP 2006 after 22 January 2016.


The key aspects of the LTIP 2006 are given below.


Individual limit


The value of an Award shall not normally exceed the executive's basic gross annual salary at the time the Performance Period commences.


Cash Awards


Awards are expressed in cash terms and do not form part of pensionable benefits. The LTIP 2006 is operated in conjunction with the EESOS. Executives are obliged to apply 30% (or such other percentage as the Committee shall determine) of the gross cash amount they receive to acquire bonus shares under the EESOS and may then receive matching shares pursuant to the rules of the EESOS. The executive receives the balance of the Award (net of income tax and employee's NIC) in cash.


Performance conditions


Payment of Awards is subject to attainment of targets set by the Committee at the time of grant which require demanding and stretching financial achievement for a fixed three-year period beginning with that financial year (the Performance Period). Once set, performance targets may not be varied by the Committee, unless the Committee reasonably considers it to be necessary to ensure that the effectiveness of the Award as an incentive is not undermined. There is no provision to retest performance.


Executives are obliged to apply 30% of any Award under the LTIP 2006 to acquire Bonus Shares under the EESOS. The Committee recognises that this may lead to its previous 20,000 matching share limit being exceeded. Accordingly, the Committee intends to allow any unused portion of that annual limit to be carried forward for a maximum of two years and thereby anticipates that this limit will only be exceeded in exceptional circumstances.


Limitations on the Issue of Shares


There is a limit of 5% of issued share capital on the number of shares which may be issued or over which rights to subscribe for new shares (Subscription Rights) may be granted under the LTSP 2008 and any other discretionary executive share option or incentive scheme of the Company in any period of ten years. There is an ultimate limit of 10% of issued share capital on the number of shares which may be issued or over which subscription rights may be granted under the LTSP 2008 and any other executive or employee share scheme established by the Company in any period of ten years. If Awards or other rights are to be satisfied by a transfer of existing shares, the percentage limits stated above will not apply insofar as it is necessary to ensure compliance with the guidelines issued from time to time by the Association of British Insurers, the percentage limits will apply to Awards or other rights satisfied by the transfer of treasury shares.

Creditor Payment Policy


It is the group's normal practice to make payments to suppliers in accordance with agreed terms provided that the supplier has performed in accordance with the relevant terms and conditions. It is not the group's policy to follow a set code or standard on payment practice.


Average creditor days outstanding at the period end for the group continuing businesses were 95 days (2008 - 34 days). This high figure reflects the situation prevalent in certain overseas territories where settlement of both creditors and debtors, where suppliers are also customers, occurs as infrequently as bi-annually or even less frequently. UK creditors are generally settled between 15 and 45 days and last year's comparatives included the wholly UK gadget business where the magnitude of its creditors influenced the calculation significantly.


There are no trade creditors in the holding company.

Corporate Governance Statement 


The Combined Code


The Board is fully committed to the highest standards of corporate governance. The Company throughout the reporting period complied with the provisions of Section 1 of the Combined Code on Corporate Governance issued by the UK Financial Services Authority.


The Board


The Board consists of a non-executive Chairman Tim Highcroft, three independent non-executive directors and two executive directors. Biographical details of the directors are given above.


The executive directors on the Board are Jim Ash, Chief Executive and Steve Oak, Group Finance Director and Company Secretary, Bob Chestnut is the recognised Senior Independent Non-Executive Director and, as such, is available to shareholders for issues that have not been resolved through the normal channels.


From time to time the Chairman meets with the other non-executive directors and, at least once a year the Senior Independent Non-Executive Director and the other non-executive directors meet without the Chairman.


The Board believes that an effective balance of power is maintained and that it is not possible for an individual or small group of individuals to dominate the Board's decision taking.


The Board has a clearly documented schedule of matters reserved for it including the agreement of strategy and the annual budget, approval of major capital expenditure, approval of any business acquisition or disposal, approval of dividends and approval of overall borrowing facilities or commitments. In addition, the separate responsibilities of the Chairman and the Chief Executive have been clearly defined and approved by the Board.


All directors have access to the advice of the Company Secretary who organises all Board matters and can only be appointed or removed by agreement of the Board as a whole. They also have access to independent professional advice where required. New directors receive induction to the group through a formal programme, which includes introductions to major shareholders as appropriate, and all directors are encouraged continually to update their skills and knowledge.


Each month members of the Board receive summary financial and operating results for each business area compared to the annual budget and the prior year. In addition, they receive a narrative review and explanation of business performance from the Chief Executive, Divisional Managing Directors and the Group Finance Director.


During 2008, a formal evaluation of individual directors and the Board as a whole was carried out. The process adopted was as follows. Three questionnaires were prepared - Board Effectiveness Evaluation, Individual Director Assessment and Assessment of the Chairman. Individually, directors completed the Board Effectiveness Evaluation questionnaire and their own Director Assessment questionnaire. The outputs were discussed on a one-to-one basis with the Chairman of the Board. The results from the questionnaires were consolidated and a report produced by the Chairman, which was discussed by the Board.


Non-executive directors also completed the Assessment of the Chairman questionnaire and met with the Senior Independent Non-Executive Director to discuss their views. The Senior Independent Non-Executive Director discussed the views of the directors (including the executive directors) with the Chairman.


The Board agreed an action plan with dates and responsibilities. Progress against the objectives has been regularly reviewed at Board meetings.


Each Committee has assessed its own work and these assessments have been considered by the Board. In the circumstances, the Board has agreed that an evaluation of the Board and its Directors will be postponed until the second half of 2010.

Internal Control


The Board recognises that it is responsible for the Company's system of internal control and for reviewing its effectiveness. Such a system can provide only reasonable and not absolute assurance against material misstatement or loss, as it is designed to manage rather than eliminate the risk of failure to achieve business objectives. The group's system as a whole is designed to provide the directors with reasonable assurance that problems are identified on a timely basis and dealt with appropriately.


During the reporting period to 30 September 2009 the group operated under an established internal control framework, key elements of which include:


Annual budgets and three-year plans are reviewed and accepted by the Board prior to implementation throughout the group


The group manages its business by monitoring performance against budget and forecast updates during the year


The group has clear procedures for the delegation of authority deriving from the list of matters reserved for the decision of the Board including the approval of capital expenditure


Each division has its own executive management meeting on a monthly basis to discuss financial and operational issues relating to the division. These meetings include reviews of reports relating to a variety of aspects of performance and control in addition, monthly meetings are held between each Managing Director and the Chief Executive and quarterly meetings are held involving all divisional Managing Directors, the Chief Executive and the Group Finance Director. Here the key performance and control points arising from individual divisions are discussed, along with broader issues relating to the group as a whole


The internal audit function regularly reviews the group's system of internal control at key operations. Corrective action is recommended, and later followed.


There is a continual process for identifying, evaluating and managing the significant risks faced by the Company and the related controls. This has been in place for the full financial period, has been reviewed by the Board and accords with the Internal Control Guidance for Directors produced by the Turnbull working party.

Audit Committee Report 


The membership of the Audit Committee consists of the independent non-executive directors with Charles Birch as chairman of the Committee.


Charles is a Chartered Accountant. The Board is satisfied in line with the Combined Code, that he has both current and relevant financial experience.


The Audit Committee met on five occasions in the reporting period. The Corporate Governance statement shows the attendance record for each member of the Committee. From time to time, the Audit Committee meets with members of the internal audit team and the external auditors without management being present.


The Audit Committee discharged its responsibilities during the reporting period by:


Agreeing the audit fee and pre-approving scope of work and the scale of fees in respect of non-audit services provided by the external auditors and ensuring that the provision of non-audit services does not impair the external auditors independence or objectivity


Discussing with the external auditors before the audit commences, the nature and scope of the audit and reviewing the auditors quality control procedures and steps taken by the auditors to respond to changes in regulatory and other requirements


Reviewing the external auditors management letter and management's response


Reviewing the internal audit programme and ensuring that the internal audit function is adequately resourced and has appropriate standing within the Company


Considering management's response to any major external or internal audit recommendations


Reviewing the Company's procedures for handling allegations from whistleblowers (details of which can be found on the Investors' menu of the Company's web-site)


Reviewing management's and the internal audit team's reports on the effectiveness of systems for internal financial control, financial reporting and risk management


Reviewing and challenging where necessary, the actions and judgements of management in relation to the interim and annual financial statements before submission to the Board


As regards the awarding of non- audit work to the external auditors, this may be awarded insofar as it does not impair the impartiality and independence of the external audit team. This work may because of external auditors knowledge of the Company's operations, include taxation compliance and planning work, and due diligence in relation to potential acquisitions. Other consultancy work for which the audit firm is considered is offered to other firms on a competitive tendering basis. If fees for such work were to exceed 20% of the audit fee in anyone year a proposal for their engagement is referred to the Audit Committee in advance of the appointment in order for the Committee to consider whether such appointment is likely to affect the audit firm's independence.


During the reporting period, the Committee reviewed its work and its terms of reference using a self-assessment process. The members of the Committee were satisfied with the workings of the Committee.
